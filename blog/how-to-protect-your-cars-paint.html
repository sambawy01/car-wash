How to Protect Your Car's Paint

Your car's paint is its first line of defence against the elements — and in Egypt's sun, dust and humidity, that defence is tested every single day. The good news is that a few simple habits will keep your paint glossy and protected for years.

Wash regularly

The foundation of paint protection is a clean surface. Dust and grime are abrasive; every time they rub against the paint — from wind, from wiping with a dry cloth — they create microscopic scratches that dull the finish. A regular wash removes these particles safely.

Wax and seal

A good wax or sealant adds a sacrificial layer between your paint and the world. It repels water, blocks UV rays, and makes the surface slick so contaminants slide off instead of bonding. Reapply every two to three months for year-round protection.

Ceramic coatings

For long-term protection, a ceramic coating bonds to the clear coat at a molecular level, lasting one to several years. It offers superior resistance to chemicals, UV and light scratches, and makes the car dramatically easier to clean. Our Polishing & Protection service includes professional-grade sealants that are difficult to apply correctly at home.

Smart habits

Park in shade where you can. Remove bird droppings and tree sap immediately — they etch into paint within hours in hot weather. Never wipe a dry car with a dry cloth; always use a detail spray or waterless wash product to lift dirt before it touches the paint. And consider a professional polish once or twice a year to remove the fine swirls that accumulate over time.
